## When a Robot Gets Kicked and Just Keeps Walking 🤖👢

Imagine strolling into a tech expo, only to impulsively kick a humanoid robot — and then watching it calmly get back up and keep walking like nothing happened! That’s exactly what happened when Zhou Hongyi (周鸿祎), the founder of 360 company, paid a visit to Songyan Power’s exhibition hall.

*Sounds like the start of a sci-fi movie, right?* But this real-life encounter with Songyan’s N2 robot quickly became a viral sensation online.

## The Charming Yet Tough N2 Robot

The N2 is not your average robot. Standing 1.2 meters tall and weighing 30 kilograms, it has 18 high-performance joints that give it an impressively human-like range of motion. Zhou Hongyi pointed out that these robots looked **more cute than creepy** — which isn't an easy feat when you’re dealing with machines that walk and run like humans.

This robot can not only walk but also ran in a world’s first half marathon for robots, securing a runner-up spot! That’s some serious stamina and coordination packed into a compact frame.

## The Kick Heard Around the Internet 🎬

Curious and cheeky, Zhou lightly kicked the N2 robot. The robot lost balance and fell, but what happened next was stunning. With the help of staff, N2 quickly got back on its feet and started walking again, as steady as ever.

Zhou humorously dubbed N2 the "most robust robot" he had ever kicked. This unexpected test not only demonstrated N2's durability but also showcased the sophistication of the engineering team behind it.

## Public Reactions: Awe and Debate

The video quickly lit up social media. On one hand, netizens marveled at N2's resilience and saw it as a glimpse into the future of intelligent robotics, sparking imaginations about AI companions and helpers becoming part of daily life.

But not everyone was amused. Some critics felt that kicking a robot might be a bit too rough, raising questions about how we should ethically treat advanced intelligent machines. This sparked lively debates online about the limits of testing and respect for AI entities.

## What Does This Mean for the Future?

The N2 robot isn’t just a cool tech demo — it symbolizes the rapid advancements in humanoid robotics that could soon impact healthcare, education, and even companionship. As companies like Songyan Power push the envelope, expect intelligent robots to become more common, reliable, and maybe even lovable.

**So next time you see a robot stumble, don’t be surprised if it gets right back up — with a little help, or maybe even on its own!**

## Final Thoughts

Zhou Hongyi’s playful kick sparked a serious conversation about how far robotics has come and where it’s headed. With innovations like N2, the boundary between humans and machines keeps getting blurrier, opening up endless possibilities — and maybe a few surprises — along the way. 🌟
