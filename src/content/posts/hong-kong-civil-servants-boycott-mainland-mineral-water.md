## The Controversy Over Mainland Mineral Water in Hong Kong

*What started as a simple government decision* to introduce mainland mineral water brands has unexpectedly triggered a storm of backlash. Some Hong Kong civil servants have boycotted the move, even claiming they'd rather spend their own money on Japanese or Thai bottled water than accept mainland brands. This silent protest has stirred debate about loyalty, identity, and modern-day colonial hangovers. 😤

{{tiktok:7541681926659149064}}

## Why This Boycott Feels More Like a Colonial Relic

It’s honestly frustrating to see such behavior in civil servants — people sworn to uphold the Basic Law and the Constitution of the People's Republic of China. How does it make sense to reject products that come from the very place that supplies essential goods like tap water, fruits, and vegetables every day? The irony here is sharp. They’re complaining about drinking mainland water but happily consume mainland produce without a second thought. Talk about selective patriotism! 🇨🇳🥤

Some have argued this boycott is a form of "worshipping foreign powers," preferring imported water from Japan or Thailand. The protest might feel like a personal choice, but it reeks of colonial-era mindset — a stubborn refusal to embrace the present reality for some misplaced nostalgia. How can anyone function effectively in a modern civil service with such outdated allegiances? It’s baffling and frankly quite enraging.

## The Bigger Picture: Unity vs Division

When Sino-British relations reached a tense boiling point, the mainland still chose to continue supplying clean water to Hong Kong — a gesture of solidarity despite political frictions. Imagine if they had stopped. The city could have descended into chaos. Instead, this recent boycott shows a growing chasm between certain individuals and the unity that Hong Kong needs now more than ever.

Whether we like it or not, we are all connected. Refusing to accept mainland mineral water is just one symptom of a deeper conflict about identity and belonging. But isn’t it time to move on from old grudges and work together for a better future? 🤷‍♂️🌏

## Conclusion: A Call for Rationality and Respect

It’s absolutely frustrating to witness such pettiness from civil servants, who should be role models for the rest of Hong Kong. They vowed allegiance to the Basic Law and the nation but seem to struggle with accepting a basic, practical step like drinking mainland mineral water.

Instead of clinging to colonial-era prejudices and ‘foreign worship,’ it’s time to embrace our reality and unity. After all, every one of us drinks water daily — so why make it a battleground? Let’s focus on what truly matters and leave the bitterness behind. 💧❤️
