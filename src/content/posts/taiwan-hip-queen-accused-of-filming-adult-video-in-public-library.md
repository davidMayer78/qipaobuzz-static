## Controversy at Kaohsiung Main Public Library: What Really Happened?

Recently, a 13-minute adult video titled *"Library Girl"* went viral, sparking a wave of concern and debate across Taiwan. Filmed at the Kaohsiung Main Public Library, the video allegedly features an internet celebrity who goes by the name "Taiwan Hip Queen," engaged in explicit acts in public areas of the library including the restroom. This incident has raised serious questions about public decency, privacy, and the effectiveness of surveillance in public spaces. 📚😳

![](/images/2025/09/TaiwanHipQueen4.webp)

## The Legal and Social Implications

The police have confirmed they are actively investigating the matter even though no formal report has yet been filed. According to Taiwanese Criminal Code, engaging in such indecent behavior in public can lead to penalties of up to one year in prison, with harsher consequences if the acts are profit-driven. Authorities have also urged the public to refrain from downloading or sharing such content to avoid legal repercussions. ⚖️🚔

This incident poses important questions: How do we balance freedom of expression with societal boundaries? Are public venues equipped to handle and prevent such provocative acts? Should laws be updated to better protect public spaces and the people who use them?

## Library's Response and Measures Moving Forward

The Kaohsiung Main Public Library stated they were unaware of the incident at the time it occurred and had not received complaints. However, they strongly condemned the behavior, citing a violation of their own Reading Regulations. To restore trust and ensure the safety and privacy of patrons, the library is enhancing its surveillance and security patrols. 🛡️📷

This situation shines a spotlight on the vulnerability of even seemingly quiet public venues. It challenges institutions everywhere to consider how best to safeguard their communities in an increasingly complex digital and social landscape.
