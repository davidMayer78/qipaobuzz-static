## Understanding the Allegations Against Peking University Professor

On August 13, the *Peking University Law School* website published an official statement addressing recent rumors circulating about their faculty member **Professor Yi Jiming (**易继明**)**. These rumors suggested improper conduct involving admissions violations and students paying for meals, which naturally raised eyebrows and concerns among the public and academic communities alike. 🧐

Such allegations can seriously undermine trust in one of China's top universities. Hence, it's critical to consider the facts carefully before jumping to conclusions. The university’s swift action signals just how seriously they view these issues.

![](/images/2025/08/PekingUniversity2.webp)

## The University's Thorough Investigation and Findings

According to the official report, Peking University took immediate steps by establishing an investigation team and gathering the necessary evidence within days of the rumors surfacing. By August 12, they had already verified the situation by interviewing involved personnel and reviewing relevant materials.

Importantly, their investigation **did not uncover any evidence** validating the claims of "students paying meal fees" or "improper admissions." This finding reassures both current and prospective students about the integrity of the university's admission practices and the ethical standards of its faculty.

![](/images/2025/08/PekingUniversity.webp)

## Zero Tolerance for Malicious Rumors and Upholding Academic Integrity

In a bold and concerned stance, the university declared it would retain the right to pursue legal action against anyone who maliciously fabricates or spreads false information. This is crucial to protect not only the reputation of the institution but also the rights of innocent individuals involved.

Peking University emphasized its commitment to *rigorous teacher ethics* and maintaining a "zero tolerance" policy towards any professional misconduct. This pledge is a reminder that academic excellence is inseparable from ethical responsibility.

## Why This Matters: The Bigger Picture

Disseminating false information online is an increasingly common challenge in today’s digital age. It can cause unwarranted damage, anxiety, and mistrust across communities. 📱💻 The response from one of China’s top universities highlights the critical need for transparency and accountability.

As concerned members of the academic community and society, it’s essential to question, verify, and seek truth rather than succumb to rumors. This incident raises important questions about how quickly misinformation can spread and the vital role institutions play in combatting it effectively.
