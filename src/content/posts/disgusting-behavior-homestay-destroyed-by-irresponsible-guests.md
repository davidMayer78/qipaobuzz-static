## The Shocking Aftermath in Zhongshan

On **August 26, 2025**, a homestay in Zhongshan, Guangdong was found in a condition that can only be described as *disgusting* and **horrifying**. Cigarette butts and betel nut remnants were scattered throughout the floors, but that was just the tip of the iceberg. The real shock came when the owner pulled back the bedding to discover stains soaked deep with spicy snack oil—hard enough to have hardened into grime! 🥵

![](/images/2025/08/DisgustingHomestyGuest1.webp)

And it didn’t stop there. Used condoms were reportedly found, broken furniture, pried-open cabinet locks, and curtains stained beyond repair. The sliding door tracks served as a convenient garbage dump. This wasn’t careless; it was absolute disrespect in physical form.

## How Could Guests Do This in Just Two Days?

This level of destruction in just *two days* baffles many. Normally, some mess is expected when people travel, but this careless behavior crosses every line of decency.
What on earth were they doing? Was this what they meant by a wild vacation? The oil-stained mattresses and pillows alone tell a scary story that no homestay owner ever wants to see. 😡

Piecing together such chaotic scenes paints a picture of guests who lacked any sense of responsibility or respect for someone else's property. And it’s not just about mess—it's the blatant disregard for communal spaces and trust.

## The Owner’s Response and the Price of Forgiveness

Despite suffering big losses (written off bedding, expensive deep cleaning, and repairs on furniture), the homestay owner accepted just **300 yuan** as compensation. 🤦‍♂️ No police involvement. It’s a cost-benefit decision that sheds light on the challenges small homestay operators face when dealing with irresponsible guests.

This compromise, though disappointing, reflects the harsh reality—pursuing legal action might cost far more than the damage itself, leading business owners to swallow their losses silently.

## Why This Should Anger Every Traveler and Host

This case isn’t just about one ruined homestay. It's a wake-up call for everyone in the travel community. **Respect and civility** should be non-negotiable. When travelers trash a place, they erode trust and potentially ruin opportunities for others who seek affordable and welcoming accommodations.

*Travelers*, think twice before turning a place into your personal garbage dump. *Hosts*, consider setting clear guidelines and screening guests carefully. Together, we can prevent this madness. ✋

## Final Thoughts: The Cost of Disrespect

Irresponsible guest behavior has real costs—not just financially, but emotionally—for those who open their homes to travelers. Let this unfortunate story remind us of all that **traveling responsibly benefits everyone**. So, pack your manners along with your suitcase and leave places better than you found them!

Because at the end of the day, no amount of spicy snack oil or broken furniture is worth the frustration and damage left behind.
