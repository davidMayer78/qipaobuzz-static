## Introduction to the 9th Danqing Youth Exhibition

*Where ink and brushes shine, youth paints with Danqing.* The 9th Danqing Youth Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Exhibition opened its doors on August 18th at the Qinhuangdao People's Art Museum in Hebei province. This event stands as one of China's most influential public welfare art activities dedicated to children and young artists, fostering creativity and cultivating traditional culture.

![](/images/2025/08/DanqingCalligraphy-1.webp)

## The Rich History and Scale of Danqing Youth Exhibition

Since its inception in 2014, the Danqing Youth Exhibition has successfully held **ten exhibitions in eight different cities**, including renowned cultural centers like Shijiazhuang, Tianjin, and Qufu. Its reach is vast: over 450,000 works have been submitted by more than 380,000 young talents nationwide. This staggering participation paints a vivid picture of how beloved and respected this event has become in the arts community.

This year alone, over 12,000 submissions poured in, painting a picture of surging enthusiasm despite strict venue limitations which meant only 370 pieces could be selected for display. These selected works represent the very best in youth creativity and dedication to Chinese calligraphy and painting.

## A Celebration Backed by Legendary Masters and Experts

The exhibition doesn’t just celebrate young talent but proudly stands on the shoulders of giants. Renowned calligraphy masters such as *Ouyang Zhongshi (*欧阳中石*), Shen Peng (*沈鹏*), and Li Duo (*李铎*)* provided inscriptions for past events, linking present-day youth with a rich cultural legacy. Additionally, over 220 prominent artists, including Xu Yu (旭宇), Zhang Xuguang (张旭光), and Du Benji (都本基), have contributed artworks and inscriptions that enhance the exhibition's academic and artistic gravitas.

This fusion of past masters’ wisdom and youthful imagination creates an inspiring atmosphere, reinforcing the exhibition's mission to both preserve and innovate traditional Chinese arts.

## Nurturing the Next Generation: Training and Public Welfare

One core philosophy of the Danqing Youth project is its **commitment to public welfare and professional cultivation**. Collaborations with prestigious organizations like the China Calligraphers Association Training Center and the Li Keran Painting Academy have introduced targeted training courses. These focus on the delicate balance of *imitation and creative expression*, essential for mastering this ancient art form while encouraging personal style.

More than 4,000 children, enthusiasts, and professional teachers have participated in these programs, making measurable contributions toward the inheritance and dynamic development of traditional Chinese calligraphy and painting.

## Looking Ahead: The Future of Danqing Youth Exhibit

According to Zhang Zhenhua (张振华), the head of the organizing committee, this year’s exhibition will delight visitors until August 25th. However, the excitement doesn’t end here. The 10th Danqing Youth Chinese Calligraphy and Painting Exhibition is already slated for August 2026 in Shijiazhuang, promising another thrilling chapter filled with creativity and cultural celebration.

## Why This Exhibition Matters

The 9th Danqing Youth Exhibition reminds us of the *power of art to connect generations* and the importance of nurturing youthful creativity. It is a vivid testament to how tradition can be kept alive, not as a museum relic but as a living, breathing, evolving culture. Watching young artists dip their brushes in ink to create stunning works is like watching a time-honored story continue to unfold with fresh energy and hope.

So, whether you are an art lover, a parent, or simply curious about Chinese culture, this exhibition provides a moving glimpse of a cultural legacy being carefully passed down — and reimagined — by the next generation.
