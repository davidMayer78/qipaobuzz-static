## Rediscovering Heritage Through Ancient Arts 🎎

*Imagine sitting quietly among bamboo groves*, plucking the strings of a **guqin** — its gentle, distant melody carrying stories ages old. This was the serene moment for Chinese youth from Japan, France, and the Netherlands at the 2025 “亲情中华·中国寻根之旅” summer camp in Shanghai. Although many were beginners, learning the basic "scattered sound" technique brought a wave of accomplishment as the iconic tune "Cang Hai Yi Sheng Xiao" echoed through the classroom.

Touching the physical thirteen "徽位" on the guqin and understanding the ancient "round heaven, square earth" philosophy embedded in its shape connected these young hearts with a deep cultural symbolism that books alone cannot convey.

![](/images/2025/08/Culture1.webp)

## Ancient Wisdom Comes Alive in Classroom 📚

The journey didn't stop at music. In the **Confucius and The Analects** class, students were introduced to one of China's most revered sages. Many had only passing notions of Confucius, but through vivid storytelling and clear explanations, they gleaned insights into his life and enduring philosophy. As the teachings of filial piety, respect, and self-cultivation came alive, the wall between past and present seemed to dissolve.

The feeling was like discovering treasured family photos in an attic, each story bringing smiles, questions, and reflections about identity and values that resonate even today.

![](/images/2025/08/Culture2.webp)

## Sensory Threads: The Art of Fragrance and Calligraphy ✒️🌿

Chinese cultural heritage thrives in smaller details, like the fragrant sachets stuffed with mugwort and lavender, crafted painstakingly by the youth. These sachets, rooted in history from *The Book of Songs* to *Dream of the Red Chamber*, became tangible keepsakes — tokens to carry home and share.

Meanwhile, the intricacies of **calligraphy** beckoned them to master brush strokes, from the patience of "reverse stroke" to the flowing "center stroke." Although their hands occasionally faltered, the triumphant sight of completed "Fu" (福) characters was a joyful milestone— a living link to ancestors' artistic expressions.

## Tea and Taiji: Embracing the Spirit of China 🍵👐

Learning to brew a fine cup of Chinese tea was another enchanting experience. Under expert guidance, the youths soaked up tea lore and etiquette, sharing sips that transcended flavor — embodying respect, harmony, and mindfulness. The mingling aromas and warm hues offered comfort like a gentle embrace.

Not to forget the lessons in martial arts where the principle "止戈为武" (stopping war is true martial arts) resonated deeply. Practicing Taiji movements demanded balance and focus, showing the power behind calmness and inner strength. It was a spirited nod to self-discipline and cultural pride.

## Bringing China’s Soul Back Home 🌏💖

Yang Xinran, a young participant from the Netherlands, expressed what many felt: "Chinese culture is profound and vast. I’ll take what I’ve learned back to share with the people around me, connecting worlds through our shared heritage." This poignant sentiment illuminated the camp’s true success— bridging continents and generations through stories, sounds, and sensations of a timeless culture.

The experience was not just a summer camp; it was a heartfelt journey that reminded everyone involved how past traditions continue to shape identity and inspire present and future dreams. 🌸
