## Heartbreaking News from Cangshan, Dali

It's with a heavy heart that we share the tragic news of an 8-year-old boy, who went missing during a summer camp activity in Cangshan, Dali, and was confirmed **dead** on the afternoon of August 13th. This boy, who faced the challenges of autism, vanished on August 9th, sparking days of intense search and rescue efforts by local authorities and volunteers.

## A Search Filled with Hope and Fear

Imagine the anguish of families and rescuers during those long days. The boy was last seen participating in a camp organized in Yunfeng Village, Wanqiao Town, an institution intended to support children with special needs such as autism, developmental delays, and hyperactivity. Sadly, the hope that kept everyone going was crushed when his body was found lifeless.💔

## Questions Surrounding the Summer Camp

What troubles me deeply is the unsettling revelation that the summer camp may not have been properly regulated. While it was approved by the market supervision department, it apparently lacked the necessary approval from the education department. This raises serious questions about the standards and oversight in place for institutions caring for vulnerable children.

Authorities are now investigating the person in charge and the teachers of the camp, as multiple departments look into possible negligence or misconduct. Families deserve transparency and safety assurances when entrusting such institutions with their children.

## Reflecting on a Community in Mourning

This tragic incident has shaken the Dali community deeply. It reminds us of the incredible responsibility society has in protecting our children, especially those with special needs who may be more vulnerable. *We must demand better protections, stricter supervision, and heartfelt care.*

May this young boy rest in peace, and may his story push us all to advocate for stronger safeguards to prevent such heartache from happening again.🙏
