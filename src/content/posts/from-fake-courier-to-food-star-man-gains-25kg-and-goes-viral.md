## The Art of the Sneaky Snack: When Hunger Meets Clever Disguise

The surveillance footage showed a man wearing a delivery platform’s cap walking into various food outlets. With an almost cartoon-like nonchalance, he pretended to check orders on his phone while grabbing the meals, then casually strolled out. Talk about multitasking! 🍕📱

Police investigations later revealed his irregular 'work hours'. When hunger hit, he’d find a mall spot, pick up some eats or a milk tea to quench his thirst, and then vanish. The brands stolen? Big names, nothing cheap — the man clearly had a taste for the good stuff 👑🥤.

## From Slim to Chubby: The Unexpected Side Effect of Food Theft

What’s even more surprising is the change in the man himself during the crime spree. About 2-3 months before his arrest, he weighed around 150-160 pounds (68-72 kg), but by the time he was caught, his weight had surged past 200 pounds (over 90 kg). That’s a whopping 25kg gain! Yes, folks — the old saying **“you are what you eat”** might just be truer than we thought 😲.

This unintended *weight gain* may be considered a hilarious example of ‘crime doesn’t pay’ in the most literal sense. And it offers a cheeky reminder that frequent indulgence, especially in fast food and sugary drinks, comes with – well – extra baggage.
